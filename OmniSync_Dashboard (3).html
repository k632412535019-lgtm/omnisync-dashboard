Why a Hub Architecture — Not Point-to-Point

In a direct mesh model, 3 systems require 3 bidirectional connections. With 3 separate marketplaces, 2 dark-store nodes, and an OMS added, this grows to 15+ point-to-point links — each with its own error handling, format mapping, and failure mode.

Industry standard (Gartner, MuleSoft, Dell Boomi): replace integration meshes with a centralised hub (iPaaS) once the system count exceeds 3. OmniSync applies this principle: one hub, governed event stream, single source of truth per domain.

Core Question 1 — Who Holds the True Inventory?

Single Source of Truth
SAP ERP vs WMS — Correct Role Definition
SAP ERP / Materials Management (MM)
  • Tracks inventory at plant / storage-location level (aggregate)
  • Financial valuation of stock (COGS, balance sheet)
  • Procurement (POs, GRNs), demand planning (MRP)
  • Owns the golden master record for every SKU
  • Does not direct warehouse execution — no bin-level visibility
WMS (SAP EWM / Standalone)
  • Tracks inventory at bin / slot / lot / serial level (granular)
  • Directs all warehouse tasks: receive, put-away, pick, pack, ship
  • De-aggregates ERP stock into physical bin assignments
  • Sole writer of on-hand stock — the only system with operational truth
  • Reports GR/GI events back to ERP at transaction confirmation points
Key insight: SAP ERP knows "Plant X has 500 units of SKU Y." WMS knows "Row 3, Bay 7, Level 2, Bin 14 holds 12 units — currently allocated to Pick Wave #441." Both views are needed; neither is a substitute for the other. WMS de-aggregates and executes; ERP plans and reports.

Why batch is cheaper than real-time (three dimensions): (1) API call volume — polling-based real-time wastes up to 90% of calls on empty checks (Integrate.io, 2025). Batch fires only on actual data. (2) Infrastructure cost — real-time requires always-on message broker nodes (Kafka/RabbitMQ) sized to peak throughput; batch uses on-demand compute sized to data volume. Confluent/Forrester TEI study (2024) documented significant cost savings for tiered vs all-real-time architectures. (3) Error-handling complexity — real-time requires per-event retry logic, dead-letter queues, idempotency keys, and continuous monitoring. Batch jobs have simple all-or-retry semantics. The engineering cost of real-time for C-class SKUs (5% of velocity) is not justified by the marginal oversell protection. Sources: Cadre Technologies (2026); Integrate.io (2025); Confluent/Forrester (2024); Salesforce EDA Decision Guide (2025)

Core Question 3 — Conflict on the Same Piece

Buffer-Protected ATS + Reservation Engine
Why True Last-Unit Races Are Structurally Rare

Marketplaces never see the safety stock. They see only ATS = On-Hand − Safety Stock − Open Reservations. The safety stock acts as a shock absorber. For a race to occur, two channels must simultaneously reduce ATS to zero — which requires draining not just the exposed ATS but fighting through the entire safety stock buffer first.

Reservation & Recovery Sequence — 5 Steps
1
Soft Reservation: Checkout places idempotent hold (TTL 10–15 min) against ATS. Retry-safe — same order-ID never creates duplicate hold.
2
Payment Confirms → Hard Allocation: Payment gateway triggers hard WMS allocation. First-confirmed-paid wins. Competing hold expires or is rejected.
3
Auto-Cancel + Instant Refund: Losing order auto-cancelled. Instant refund triggered. No manual review. WMS pick task proceeds for winner.
4
Customer Recovery: Losing customer notified immediately with substitute SKU + restock ETA from ERP open PO data. Protects NPS and rating.
5
B2B Priority Override: Priority Score = (SLA Slack)⁻¹ × Penalty. B2B with 2h cutoff + ₫5M penalty beats B2C with 3-day window — regardless of payment sequence.
